Papers have a fixed format: PDF, two columns, static figures. That format is right for
archival publication and wrong for a lot of technical communication, especially anything
where the reader should be able to *touch the parameters*: interventions on model
internals, sweeps over hyperparameters, threshold choices in an evaluation.

This section of the site holds research notes written for the web. The commitments:

1. **Permanent URLs.** Each note lives at a stable address and states its publication
   date. Substantive revisions are dated in place.
2. **Citable.** Each note ships citation metadata and a BibTeX entry (below). These
   notes are self-published and not peer-reviewed; cite them as what they are.
3. **Interactive where it matters.** Figures are components, not screenshots, when the
   point of the figure is a parameter the reader should explore.
